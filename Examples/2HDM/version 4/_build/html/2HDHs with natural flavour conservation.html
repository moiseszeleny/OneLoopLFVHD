Lagrangian¶

We will in this work consider that there is no CP violation in the vacuum expectation values (vevs) of the scalar doublets \(\Phi_{1,2}\) as a consecuance \(v_{1,2}\) are reals. Thus

\[\begin{split} \Phi_{j}=\left(\begin{array}{c} \phi_{j}^{+} \\ \left(v_{j}+\rho_{j}+i \eta_{j}\right) / \sqrt{2} \end{array}\right) \end{split}\]

with \(v_1 = v \cos{\beta}\) and \(v_2 = v \sin{\beta}\).

The neutral scalar are \(h\) and \(H\) with \(m_h < m_H\) which are orthogonal combinations of \(\rho_1\) and \(\rho_2\)

(1)¶\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}h =\rho_{1} \sin{\alpha} -\rho_{2} \cos{\alpha}\\H =-\rho_{1} \cos{\alpha} -\rho_{2} \sin{\alpha}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

Notice that the Standard-Model Higgs boson would be

(2)¶\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} H^{\mathrm{SM}} &=\rho_{1} \cos \beta+\rho_{2} \sin \beta \\ &=h \sin (\alpha-\beta)-H \cos (\alpha-\beta) \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

To other side, we choose \(\alpha\) and \(\beta \) in the first quadrant, also \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) non-negative without loss of generality.

Following the notation of Aoki et. al. [AKTY09], we define the parameters \(\xi_h^f\), \(\xi_H^f\), \(\xi_A^f\) through the Yukawa Lagrangian

(3)¶\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}_{\text {Yukawa }}^{2 \mathrm{HDM}}=&-\sum_{f=u, d, \ell} \frac{m_{f}}{v}\left(\xi_{h}^{f} \bar{f} f h+\xi_{H}^{f} \bar{f} f H-i \xi_{A}^{f} \bar{f} \gamma_{5} f A\right) \\ &-\left\{\frac{\sqrt{2} V_{u d}}{v} \bar{u}\left(m_{u} \xi_{A}^{u} \mathrm{P}_{L}+m_{d} \xi_{A}^{d} \mathrm{P}_{R}\right) d H^{+}+\frac{\sqrt{2} m_{\ell} \xi_{A}^{\ell}}{v} \overline{\nu_{L}} \ell_{R} H^{+}+\text {H.c. }\right\} \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

where \(P_{L/R}\) are projection operators for left-/right-handed fermions, and the factors \(\xi\) for charged leptons are presented in the table xi_factors(for the case of \(\xi\) factors for quarks see [BFL+12]).
